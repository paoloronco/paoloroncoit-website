## Design Context

### Users

The portfolio addresses a mixed audience: potential product users and clients, recruiters, and technical peers. Project pages must let a non-technical visitor understand the product and its value immediately, while still giving recruiters and specialists enough evidence to assess the work.

### Brand Personality

Direct, credible, and product-led. The voice is confident without being promotional, explains concrete value before implementation details, and presents each project as a real product rather than defining it through competitors.

### Aesthetic Direction

An editorial, dark technical portfolio with concise case-file structure and strong information hierarchy. Content should use progressive disclosure: an accessible product overview first, capabilities and adoption choices second, and technical depth last.

### Design Principles

1. State what the product is and who it helps before explaining how it was built.
2. Lead with concrete outcomes and recognizable use cases, not competitor comparisons.
3. Write the first layer for everyone; place technical evidence in a clearly separated final layer.
4. Make every section answer one question and remove repeated claims.
5. Keep terminology consistent across Italian and English versions.
